What is Starknet

Starknet is a permissionless Validity-Rollup (also known as a “ZK-Rollup”) that supports general computation and currently operates in production as an L2 network over Ethereum. The eventual L1 security of Starknet is ensured by its use of the safest and most scalable cryptographic proof system – STARK.

Starknet contracts are (for the most part) written in the Cairo language – A Turing complete programming language designed for STARK proofs.

Why Starknet

Imagine you're baking a delicious cake. In the world of Ethereum, baking this cake requires a lot of effort and time. You need to mix the ingredients, put the cake in the oven, and wait for it to bake. This is similar to how Ethereum works, where each transaction (like baking a cake) takes a lot of computational power and time on the main Ethereum network (Layer 1). Now, let's introduce Starknet. Think of Starknet as a magical kitchen that helps you bake cakes in a smarter way. Instead of baking each cake individually, Starknet allows you to create a special recipe (a proof) that proves your cake has been baked correctly. You do this by baking one cake and showing the recipe to others. In the context of Ethereum, this means that instead of doing heavy computations directly on the main Ethereum network (Layer 1), like baking the cake from scratch, Starknet lets you do the hard work off-chain (like baking the cake once) and then provides a special proof (recipe) to the Ethereum network. This proof is much smaller and easier to verify. Others on the Ethereum network can quickly check the proof (just like looking at the recipe) to make sure the cake (transaction or computation) has been done correctly. This way, the Ethereum network doesn't have to do all the heavy baking itself; it just needs to verify the proof. So, Starknet is like a smart kitchen that lets you bake cakes (perform computations) off-chain and then show a proof (recipe) to the Ethereum network (Layer 1) to quickly and efficiently confirm that everything is correct. It helps Ethereum scale by reducing the work the network has to do while keeping everything secure and efficient, just like having a cake recipe that everyone can trust!
